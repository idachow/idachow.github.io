Playing with Interactivity

A few kits and tools that allow you to get going with IoT right out of the box, and also help with understanding the basics of circuits and electronics.

LittleBits

Connecting pieces that allow you to create circuits without worrying about the technicalities of resistors, capacitors, soldering, etc.

MaKey MaKey

Get creative with how your can twist the idea of a "circuit" with anything that conducts -- your body, fruit, water... check out a video:


The MaKey MaKey puts you into the circuit, and once you touch a conductive object, you complete the circuit and can trigger responses on the computer. Explore how you can spatially reconceptualize existing games, programs, and more! Check out more details on how to get started on the MaKey MaKey website.

Resistors, Capacitors, Transistors

These components get used on a case-by-case basis in your circuit - to resist the flow of current (resistors), temporarily store electric charge (capacitors), and control two different flows of current (transistors). [Read more at the source]

LEDs & Diodes

Diodes take away from the power, but do not decrease current. LEDs are one form of diode, and stand for "Light-emitting Diode".

Programming & Microprocessors

Gaining more control over all the details.

Processing

A Java-based programming language that caters towards graphics and graphics-related libraries. Check out Processing.

Arduino

A microcontroller with a great programming system behind it - with integration with other languages like Processing. Check out the Arduino board.

Other boards

Other boards that follow along the lines of Arduino are the Intel Atom Board, the Raspberry Pi, and the Sparkfun Red Board... all branded a bit differently, but technically able to all do the same thing.

Integration with sensors & more

Once you can program and complete circuits with given parts, you can experiment with buying more parts from places like Sparkfun which could be anything from motors and photocells to DS touch screens, and you can play with taking apart toys and old computers and see what you can create!
