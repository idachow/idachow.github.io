navigift: a gift giving experience

Medium, tools, and skills: Photoshop, Illustrator, Invision, Mobile Application Design

When & where: Fall 2015, Carnegie Mellon University, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

Summary: This was a class project where we were to transform a text corpus on "Industrial Ecology" into a print booklet. I decided to associate the text corpus with the Whole Foods brand, and wanted to convey a sense of the farm-to-table ethos Whole Foods embodies.

Goal: Create a printed booklet that ties together the given text corpus with a brand to create a visual story.

Reflection

As the first print project I had done, I struggled a bit with the inherent design differences between digital and print. With your average digital screen, small fonts and muted colors play well into clean and easy-on-the-eyes designs. This is not the case with print, where it is no longer to easy to read 9pt fonts and gray and brown letters are much harder to decipher. I printed my booklet twice before coming up with the final design, noticing only some issues when holding the printed product in my hands. I learned a lot by working digitally on a handheld printed product, and appreciate InDesign's flexibility and many options that I could utilize into making my booklet look the way I wanted it.
