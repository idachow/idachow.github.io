process of yeats mural

Medium, tools, and skills: Acrylic, mural painting

When & where: Spring 2014, Lincoln High School, Portland, Oregon

Summary: Process photos of how I painted a mural to backdrop an English classroom, illustrating some of my favorite poems by William Butler Yeats.



I first drew up multiple sketches in Photoshop, and worked with my English teacher to pick one he liked best.





Following my digital sketch, I mapped out my sketch onto the old chalkbaord.





I moved from the sketch to painting by painting blocks of the background.





Since I was painting with acrylics, the short drying time led to a lot of repainting and so the process of painting and blending in took awhile.









Once finished, I found that the varnish I applied on top reflected glare from the sun (the classroom faced west) meant the mural could only fully be seen (in true colors) from an angle - coincidentally the angle from which my teacher sits at his desk. To this point, I would definitely be more mindful of topcoats and varnishes from now on, especially on a immovable work like a mural.

In the dim glass the demons hold,
The glass of outer weariness,
